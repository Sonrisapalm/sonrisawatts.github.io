Research Results in St. Kitts and Nevis: Parents, Teachers, and AI in the Classroom

As part of my research initiative Emotion Encoded, I asked participants a simple but important question:
“Would you be comfortable if your child’s teacher used AI to help with grading or lesson planning?”

The responses depicted something powerful:

  • 53.7% said “yes” but only under conditions — parents want AI as an assistant, with teacher review on every decision.
  • 26% said “no” — expressing resistance and lack of trust in AI for formative education.
  • 11% said “yes” without conditions — seeing AI simply as a tool, not a threat.

Conditional trust signals a bigger message: AI integration in education requires understanding human emotional reasoning. Concepts like Status Quo Bias, Automation Bias, Algorithm Aversion, and Framing Effects may influence trust.

Overall, this echoes a larger theme in Emotion Encoded: AI is not only about performance; it’s about how people emotionally negotiate trust with these systems.
