The "Convincing" Trap: Why Dr. Navi Muradali Thinks AI Needs a Human Co-Pilot

Dr. Navi Muradali

Founder & CEO, Patient Connect | Former Mayor of San Fernando

In the latest installment of the Emotion Encoded expert series, I spoke with Dr. Navi Muradali about the friction between clinical intuition and algorithmic advice. It focused on the reality of medical accountability and why "Explainable AI" might actually create new risks for seasoned professionals.

The Interview

1. On Trust: What do you think is the main reason doctors reject AI?
"I don’t think it’s just about trust in the data; it’s really about responsibility. At the end of the day, the physician is the one accountable for the outcome, so there’s always going to be some hesitation in relying on a system you didn’t personally train or fully understand."
2. Explainable AI: Could 'Explainable AI' actually make it harder for a doctor to spot a mistake?
"That’s actually a real concern. Just because an explanation sounds convincing doesn’t mean it’s right. In the systems we’re developing, we try not to present AI as giving a final answer, but rather showing trends and signals that the physician can interpret."
3. On the Final Call: Will the doctor always be the sole decision-maker?
"I think the physician will still make the final call. AI acts more like a co-pilot. It helps you see what’s coming, but the responsibility for the decision still sits with the physician."
Dr. Muradali’s insights highlight a critical pivot: the move from "trusting the tech" to "retaining accountability." The human expert must remain the final filter.
