When Lawyers Use AI Without Telling You

🤗 St. Kitts & Nevis 🤗

What happens to trust when professionals rely on AI but don’t disclose it? In a survey of 68 people, Emotion Encoded asked: “If your lawyer relied on an AI tool in your case without informing you, how would that affect your trust?”

  • 39.7% said they would be fine with it only if the lawyer still used their own judgment
  • 22.1% felt uncomfortable with the lack of transparency
  • 13.2% said they would feel angry or betrayed
  • 11.8% weren’t surprised, assuming AI already operates behind the scenes

The strongest message is clear: Accuracy alone doesn’t build trust. An AI tool can statistically be 99.9% correct in legal analysis, but for everyday people, transparency and human oversight are what truly matter.

Psychology Terms that Apply

  • Automation Bias: The opposite risk — blindly accepting AI outputs without questioning them.
  • Framing Effect: People respond differently depending on the wording. “AI support for lawyers” feels safer than “AI decision-making.”

Why This Matters for Domain Professionals

In high-stakes fields like law, clients aren’t just seeking efficiency. They want honesty, accountability, and reassurance that human reasoning is still central. AI doesn’t erase the need for human judgment — it makes it more valuable.
