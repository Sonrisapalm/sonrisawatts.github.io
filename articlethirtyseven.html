The Legal Black Box: Insights from Sasha Lloyd

I asked Sasha Lloyd, a young lawyer, about the "black box" of AI in professional fields. Her insights highlight a crucial tension: in law, a fast answer is worthless if you cannot verify the path taken to reach it.

Transparency as a Requirement
"Transparency in legal reasoning is non-negotiable; you need to see the path, not just the destination. The practical concern is that most authoritative legal resources are not freely accessible. An AI confidently citing a case is one thing, but whether it can actually link you to a verified, full text source is a very different matter."

Clinical Intuition vs. The Algorithm: Dr. Sharon Cordner

In internal medicine, a diagnosis is a weight carried by a human being. I spoke with Dr. Sharon Cordner to explore the tension between a doctor's seasoned intuition and the cold objectivity of AI.

The Emotional Alarm

When an algorithm contradicts a doctor’s gut feeling, it triggers an immediate sense of responsibility.

"My first emotion would be concern. I would be concerned that the AI could be misleading less informed people. Sometimes, we use AI for a quick summary, so if the response is not in line with medical reality, I would be concerned."
Interrogation over Acceptance
"A physician should trust collaboration with a team, and use AI for a differential which must be interrogated and substantiated. You really do need to read the studies, or look at evidence before accepting AI conclusions as they might also be biased."
The Shadow of Complacency

Dr. Cordner warns that leaning too hard on the machine might cause us to lose the very drive to learn from experience.

"It could reshape the experience by causing complacency and laziness. Relying on AI will change how a physician might otherwise learn from experience."

This is the core of Article 37. Whether in Law or Medicine, the human element—the ability to interrogate and protect—is what keeps the system honest.
