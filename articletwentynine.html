AI in the Classroom: Guidance from Dr. Sarah Owen

Emotion Encoded Research Initiative — Dr. Sarah Owen, Educator • November 4, 2025

Dr. Sarah Owen argues that artificial intelligence can be a powerful classroom assistant when used deliberately. AI should set the foundation for tasks like lesson planning and personalized learning, while teachers apply their curricular expertise to adapt outputs for local needs. Across the Caribbean, contextual fit matters: datasets that power AI often miss socio-linguistic subtleties and cultural knowledge, so relying on raw AI output risks producing educational materials that do not match learners' realities.

AI as a Foundation

"Use AI to start work, not finish it. Let teachers shape and verify outputs so learning objectives stay central."

Cultural Context Matters

AI draws from global data that may not include Caribbean contexts. Customization is essential to avoid misalignment and misinformation.

Guarding Creativity

Beware homogenization. Overreliance risks creating a "digital colonization" in which dominant cultures shape learning for others.

Practical Advice for Teachers

  • Start small: use AI to draft lesson outlines and save time on preliminary research.
  • Customize and verify: always adapt AI-generated material to local learning goals and cultural norms.
  • Teach students to use AI as a scaffold — not a substitute — encouraging critical thinking and original work.

Notable Quotes

“AI can provide a useful starting point, but experience and expertise must guide how we teach and learn." — Dr. Sarah Owen
"Cultures that are more digitally literate will create the tools used by the less literate — customization prevents a new form of colonization." — Dr. Sarah Owen

Conclusion

Dr. Owen emphasizes balance: embrace AI for efficiency but safeguard pedagogy, culture, and student agency. For Caribbean education systems, that balance requires intentional adaptation, teacher training, and policy guidance that preserves local knowledge while responsibly integrating new tools.
