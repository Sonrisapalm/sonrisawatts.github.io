Shaping Artificial Intelligence people can trust by first understanding the psychological factors that shape human perception of it. By conducting interviews with experts in law, medicine/healthcare, finance, and education, and gaining perceptions and insight from the general population from and working in the Caribbean Region, Emotion Encoded helps to align AI systems with the human mind, not just with data, ensuring their effective adoption.

Founded by Sonrisa Watts at 24 years old (July 2025) in St. Kitts & Nevis, following the completion of my Psychology BSc degree. This research initiative ran for 6-7 months and will end in a few months. All interviews (anonymous or not) and research results will be posted to my website eventually.

Emotion Encoded is an independent research initiative. I investigate how trust, cognitive bias, heuristics, and judgment distortions shape AI integration into high-stakes domains.

I am dedicated to using Psychology to Decode Emotional Barriers to AI Adoption.

I had no institutional backing, no team, and no funding: just agency and drive.

I founded this project because I needed research experience but had no access to traditional research opportunities, mentors or labs.

Human Judgment & AI Trust

Epistemia: The feeling of knowing without evaluation. Fluent language creates a credibility bias where humans over-believe AI.

This framework closely mirrors my research on trust calibration and metacognitive dissonance in high-stakes contexts.

View Research Paper

The paper identifies breakdowns in grounding, experience, motivation, causality, metacognition, and value ; showing why humans systematically over-believe LLM outputs. Fluent language creates a credibility bias, producing the feeling of knowing without epistemic evaluation, a condition the authors term Epistemia.

This framework closely mirrors my research on trust calibration, metacognitive dissonance, and how psychological heuristics shape human reliance on AI systems in high-stakes contexts.
